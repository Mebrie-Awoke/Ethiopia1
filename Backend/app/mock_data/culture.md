# Ethiopian Culture

## Cultural Diversity and Collective Identity

Ethiopia is home to more than 80 ethnic groups, each with distinct customs, languages, and artistic traditions. Yet there is a strong sense of shared identity grounded in hospitality, respect for elders, and the importance of community.

## Music, Dance, and Oral Tradition

Music is central to Ethiopian life. Traditional instruments such as the krar, masenqo, and begena accompany songs that tell stories of love, history, and faith. Dance forms vary by region, with energetic shoulder movements in the north, ritual performances in the south, and celebratory group dances at festivals.

## Coffee Ceremony

The Ethiopian coffee ceremony is a daily ritual of hospitality and connection. Green beans are roasted over open flame, ground by hand, and brewed in a jebena. Guests gather to share three rounds of coffee, accompanied by popcorn or bread, while conversations flow.

## Clothing, Jewelry, and Craftsmanship

Traditional dress is expressed through woven cotton garments called habesha kemis for women and gabi or netela shawls. Intricate silver jewelry, leatherwork, pottery, and basket weaving represent centuries of craftsmanship passed down through families.

## Festivals and Public Celebrations

Festivals are moments of national pride and spiritual renewal. Timkat, the celebration of Epiphany, features colorful processions, choir singing, and ritual baptisms. Meskel commemorates the finding of the True Cross with bonfires and joyful gatherings.

## Cuisine and Communal Dining

Ethiopian meals are shared from a large platter, usually based on injera, a sourdough flatbread made from teff. Dishes such as doro wat, kitfo, and shiro are layered on injera, and diners eat together with their right hand, symbolizing unity and generosity.

## Art and Literature

Ethiopian art includes illuminated manuscripts, church murals, and contemporary paintings inspired by history and spirituality. Literature ranges from ancient religious texts written in Ge’ez to modern novels and poetry exploring national memory, social change, and identity.
