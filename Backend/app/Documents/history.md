# Ethiopian History

## Ancient Origins and the Birth of Civilization

Ethiopia is one of the oldest nations in the world, with human history stretching back more than three million years. The ancient kingdom of Aksum emerged around the first century CE in what is now northern Ethiopia and Eritrea. Aksum became a powerful trading state, connecting Africa, Arabia, and the Mediterranean. Its wealth came from frankincense, myrrh, ivory, gold, and agricultural products.

## The Rise of Aksum and Christianity

Aksum adopted Christianity in the 4th century under King Ezana, becoming one of the first states in the world to embrace the faith. The Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo Church remains central to Ethiopian identity today, with unique liturgy, religious art, and monastic traditions.

## Medieval Kingdoms and the Solomonic Dynasty

Following the decline of Aksum, medieval Ethiopia saw the rise of several powerful kingdoms, including Gondar, Gojjam, and the Zagwe dynasty. The Solomonic dynasty claimed descent from King Solomon and the Queen of Sheba. Its rulers built monumental churches, rock-hewn sanctuaries, and enduring symbols of Ethiopian statehood.

## Adwa and the Global Struggle Against Colonialism

In 1896, Emperor Menelik II led Ethiopian forces to victory over Italian invaders at the Battle of Adwa. This historic victory preserved Ethiopian independence and inspired anti-colonial movements across Africa and beyond.

## The Modern Era and Emperor Haile Selassie

Haile Selassie ruled Ethiopia from 1930 to 1974, ushering in a period of modernization while navigating colonial pressures and global diplomacy. Ethiopia joined the League of Nations and later became a founding member of the United Nations and the African Union.

## Revolution, Derg, and Federalism

A military junta known as the Derg took power in 1974, initiating a socialist era that lasted until 1991. During this period, Ethiopia underwent dramatic social and economic change. In 1991, a federal system was established to recognize Ethiopia's ethnic and linguistic diversity.

## Contemporary Ethiopia

Today, Ethiopia is a rapidly developing nation with a vibrant civil society, expanding infrastructure, and a growing digital economy. Its history of independence, cultural resilience, and religious diversity continues to shape a modern national identity founded on tradition and innovation.
