# Ethiopian Regions

## The Highlands and the Northern Plateau

The Ethiopian Highlands are often called the "Roof of Africa." The northern plateau includes historic cities such as Gondar, Lalibela, and Aksum. This region is famous for its mountain scenery, ancient churches, and cooler climate.

## The Rift Valley and Lakes

The Rift Valley runs through Ethiopia and contains a chain of freshwater lakes, hot springs, and volcanic formations. This area is home to many of Ethiopia's unique ecosystems and provides fertile land for agriculture.

## The Southern Nations and Cultural Diversity

The Southern Nations, Nationalities, and Peoples' Region (SNNPR) includes dozens of ethnic groups and languages. It is a center of cultural diversity, traditional farming, music, and ritual life.

## The Eastern Lowlands and Pastoral Communities

The eastern lowlands are home to pastoralist communities such as the Afar and Somali peoples. The landscape ranges from desert to salt flats, including the Danakil Depression, one of the hottest places on earth.

## The Capital Region

Addis Ababa is the national capital and a major economic, political, and cultural hub. It serves as the seat of the African Union and hosts embassies, universities, and museums.

## Regional Identity and Local Governance

Ethiopia's federal system recognizes regional autonomy. Each region has its own administration, language policies, and cultural institutions while participating in national governance.
